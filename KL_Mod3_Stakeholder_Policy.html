DFSA Knowledge Library · ILAM Module 3 — Participatory

Who holds the levers, and does the system enable change?

Stakeholder analysis and policy-institutional assessment — the foundational read on the actors, interests and rules that decide whether landscape interventions can actually work.

Even the best-targeted intervention fails if the wrong actors are missed or the enabling environment blocks it. This assessment is the foundational analytical step of the participatory module: it maps who matters and tests whether the policy, legal and institutional setting helps or hinders sustainable land management.

Two linked analyses

Stakeholder analysisIdentify actors across levels; map their interests, influence and relationships; locate champions, gatekeepers and the under-represented.
Policy & institutional assessmentReview the policy, legal and institutional framework for the mandates, gaps, overlaps and incentives that shape implementation.

How it works

The method draws on field evidence, baseline assessments, capacity-needs analyses, stakeholder-engagement records and policy reviews across the six programme countries. It is designed as a standalone technical reference for programme staff, national counterparts and landscape partners — and to be read with the monitoring and adaptive-management modules, which it sets up.

Why it matters

It makes the institutional network legible

The actor map is the human counterpart to the platform’s network map — the ministries, agencies, producer bodies and partners whose relationships determine whether practices spread or stall.

It surfaces the binding constraints

By naming policy gaps, overlapping mandates and missing incentives early, it lets the programme design around the real obstacles rather than discovering them mid-implementation.
