Sand dams

A wall that traps river sand and stores water in the pore spaces

What it is

A sand dam is a reinforced wall built across an ephemeral sandy river that traps the coarse sand carried down by successive floods. Water is stored in the voids between the sand grains behind the dam, protected from evaporation, and drawn from a well.

It is one of the most elegant ideas in water harvesting: the river builds its own reservoir out of sand, and the stored water keeps for months because the sand shields it from the sun.

How it works

  • Site on bedrock across a seasonal sandy stream of low gradient
  • Build the wall in stages so each flood deposits coarse sand (not silt) behind it
  • The sand body fills with water each rains
  • Abstract via a drain/sump and hand-pump; community-built and managed

Key facts
