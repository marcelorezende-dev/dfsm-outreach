DFSA Knowledge Library · ILAM Module 1 — Land Condition

Is the land getting greener or browner?

How the programme reads land productivity from satellites — the NDVI-based Trajectory and State indicators that underpin land-degradation-neutrality monitoring under the United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification (UNCCD).

Land productivity is one of the three UNCCD indicators of land degradation. The programme derives it from annual Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) (a satellite measure of vegetation greenness) over 16-year windows, at thousands of permanent sampling plots — turning a long, noisy time-series into a clear, defensible verdict on whether each location is improving, stable, or degrading.

Two complementary indicators

TrajectoryThe direction & significance of the trend, via Theil–Sen slope estimation and the Mann–Kendall test.
StateWhether the most recent 3 years depart significantly from the preceding 13-year baseline.
PerformanceProductivity relative to similar land units (handled separately, via land-use management units).

How it works

NDVI is drawn from the MODIS product (250 m, 16-day composites) and summed annually for each Collect Earth plot. Both indicators use a 16-year interval ending the year before the reporting year, following the UNCCD convention — so a 2025 report uses 2009–2024. Categories run from strong degradation to strong improvement, anchoring every plot to an interpretable label.

The finding that matters

Reporting-year choice changes the verdict

Sliding the window by a single year can flip a plot's classification. Across Zimbabwe's project area the data show a real shift from degradation toward improvement — but Trajectory responds smoothly while State spikes on individual high-greenness years.

So: report on multiple periods

A one-off snapshot can mislead. The method's innovation is to compute the indicators across several consecutive windows and read the pattern — making land-productivity monitoring robust rather than an artefact of when the report happened to be written.

Where it connects

Land-productivity maps flag where condition is changing; the Simplified Land Degradation Assessment in Drylands (LADA) ground-truthing explains why, the value-chain and Forest and Farm Producer Organization (FFPO) work decide what to do, and the carbon tools estimate the benefit. Productivity is the entry point of the whole assessment chain.
