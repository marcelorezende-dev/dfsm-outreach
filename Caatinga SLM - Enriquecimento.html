Enriquecimento

Enrichment seeding of degraded Caatinga that has lost its herb layer

What it is

Where indiscriminate use has stripped the native herbaceous diversity, enrichment restores forage by minimum-tillage reseeding of adapted native and/or hardy forage species, keeping 150–200 trees per hectare and correcting the soil’s chronic phosphorus deficit (Araújo Filho, 2013). It delivers the highest carrying capacity of any managed Caatinga (Araújo Filho, 2013).

How it works

  • Thin the woody layer to 150–200 trees/ha in the dry season
  • Sow forage in the rains by minimum tillage, preserving the native herb layer
  • Apply around 100 kg/ha P₂O₅ (phosphorus is usually the limiting nutrient) (Araújo Filho, 2013)
  • Weed and cut regrowth as needed; grasses such as buffel, capim-corrente, gramão Aridus

Key facts

Phytomass
~8,000 kg/ha/yr
Available forage
~4,800 kg/ha/yr
Carrying capacity
1.1 cattle or 10 small-stock /ha/yr
Live-weight
172 (cattle) / 120 (goats) / 180 (sheep) kg/ha/yr
Cost : benefit
≈ 1 : 2.5

Figures from Araújo Filho (2013).

Note

Enrichment is profitable for both breeding and growing-out phases; the cost:benefit of 1:2.5 was measured at Embrapa Caprinos, Sobral.
