DFSA Knowledge Library · Gender — WeCaN

Women’s knowledge, made to count

WeCaN — the Community of Knowledge Practice for Women in Dryland Forests and Agrosilvopastoral Systems — is a platform for women’s empowerment in dryland regions: a space to connect, share best practices, and be heard at local, regional and global level.

WeCaN is a Community of Practice that links existing national and regional women’s networks through one platform, letting them share knowledge, lessons learned and experiences across South–South Cooperation countries. Its members are focal points from grassroots and women’s organizations, civil society, community-based organizations, policymakers, practitioners, public institutions and researchers — all committed to gender-responsive approaches in dryland areas.

What WeCaN does

Share best practicesWorks with women’s communities to share best practices, lessons learned and innovative solutions to common challenges.
Capitalise on women’s knowledgeHighlights the unique solutions women hold to dryland problems, and empowers them to build on their knowledge of natural-resource management.
Strengthen capacitiesTraining sessions and webinars boost advocacy skills so women can make their voices heard on the global stage.
Speed up policyCreates actions and proposals to counter common advocacy challenges and accelerate the policy process.

Where it sits in DFSA

The living gender network

WeCaN is the standing community of women practitioners behind DFSA’s gender work — complementing the When Women Lead story and the Global Gender Action Plan with people, not just policy.

From grassroots to global

By linking national and regional networks, WeCaN carries field knowledge up to regional and global advocacy — the same flow DFSA maps as a living network of connected parts.
