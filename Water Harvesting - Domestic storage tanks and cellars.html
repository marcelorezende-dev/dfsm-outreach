Domestic storage tanks & cellars

Covered tanks and underground cellars for household runoff

What it is

Domestic storage tanks & cellars are the covered tanks and underground "water cellars" that store harvested roof, yard or road runoff so a household or small farm can draw on it across the dry season. They range from small plastic tanks beside the house to large reinforced-concrete cellars dug into the ground, and they are what turns intermittent rainfall into a year-round supply (Mekdaschi Studer & Liniger, 2013).

In the WOCAT classification the store is part of the rooftop / courtyard water harvesting group. At scale the impact is large: in China these cellars and tanks have supplied drinking water to some 15 million people and helped irrigate about 1.2 million ha – a household technique that adds up to a regional water source.

How it works

  • Lead runoff through coarse filtration / sedimentation into a covered store so silt and debris settle out before storage
  • Choose the material by cost and durability – masonry, reinforced concrete, ferro-cement, plastic or geo-fabric
  • Size by use – a smaller tank for drinking, a larger cellar where the store also irrigates
  • Draw the water by hand-pump or small pump
  • Keep the store covered to cut evaporation and contamination
