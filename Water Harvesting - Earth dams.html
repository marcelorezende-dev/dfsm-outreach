Earth dams

Earth embankment dams impounding seasonal wadi flow

What it is

Earth dams are compacted earth embankments built across a wadi or valley to impound seasonal runoff into a reservoir for irrigation, livestock watering and groundwater recharge – the largest of the on-farm storage structures.

They store the most water of any of these techniques, but demand the most care: a protected spillway, sound compaction and regular de-silting are what keep an earth dam safe and useful.

How it works

  • Build a keyed, compacted earth embankment across the watercourse
  • Provide a protected spillway and an outlet
  • Manage sedimentation and seepage
  • Boost usable capacity by moving stored water into the soil profile / aquifer
