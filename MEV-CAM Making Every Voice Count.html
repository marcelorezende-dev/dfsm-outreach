Monitoring in the community’s own voice

MEV-CAM — Making Every Voice Count for Adaptive Management — brings policymakers and rural communities together to deepen understanding of dryland production systems: integrated landscape and forest management, restoration, food security and land degradation neutrality.

Communication, monitoring and evaluation are usually done through words — we write, present, report and disseminate, mostly vocally or on paper. But the people for whom this information is most relevant — the members of dryland communities — may not be served by these tools. Written texts matter, yet other forms of communication should count too. The most important of these is participatory video.

How participatory video works

Training on the groundThrough participatory-video training, MEV-CAM empowers rural communities to document and share their knowledge, experience and best practices in dryland landscape management.
Filmed by the communityVideos taken by active community members involved in restoration capture the real situation in remote dryland areas — not a visitor’s account.
Shared widelySharing these videos spreads best practices among other communities worldwide affected by the same issues.
