Confirming what the satellites see

The Simplified LADA — a fast, plot-based field assessment that anchors satellite signals to on-the-ground reality, and explains the causes of degradation that no image can reveal.

Remote sensing shows what is changing; it cannot show why. Adapted from the LADA-WOCAT methodology, the Simplified LADA sends trained observers to the exact Collect Earth permanent plots used for satellite classification, to record the direct causes and indirect drivers of degradation, the conservation responses already in place, and whether they are working.

A three-step field protocol

1 · CharacterizeLand degradation type, degree, direct causes and indirect drivers.
2 · Document responseConservation measures in place, by group, and their effectiveness.
3 · PhotographStandardized photo monitoring — a permanent visual time-series at each plot.

Officers navigate to each plot by GPS, observe directly, and interview local stakeholders — often through a participatory multi-stakeholder workshop. The result is a bidirectional validation: field findings refine the satellite classification, and the classification frames the field reporting.

Why it matters

It explains the map

The driver analysis feeds focus-group design and the policy-institutional assessment — turning “this area is degrading” into “here is what is driving it and what response is working.”

It feeds carbon & monitoring

Its soil-type classification is a required input to the EX-ACT/NEXT carbon balance, and its repeat photos give the simplest, most credible evidence of change between assessment cycles.
