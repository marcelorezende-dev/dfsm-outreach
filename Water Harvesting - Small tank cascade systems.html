Small tank cascade systems

Chains of reservoirs down a valley, each feeding the next

What it is

A small tank cascade system is an ancient landscape of interconnected man-made reservoirs ("tanks") arranged down a valley so the overflow of each feeds the next, capturing seasonal rain across a whole micro-watershed for irrigation, recharge and domestic use.

It is water harvesting at the scale of a whole valley – a heritage of the Sri Lankan Dry Zone, governed for centuries by community rules and seasonal maintenance.

How it works

  • Build/maintain a chain of tanks down the catchment
  • Each tank’s spillway feeds the one below
  • Sediment-trapping tree/grass strips and a community water-warden ("Vew Vidane") govern sharing
  • De-silt the tanks in the dry season
