# Acronyms — house style

FAO style: **spell an acronym out in full on its first mention in a page's body
prose, with the acronym in parentheses. After that, use the acronym alone.**

> …reported through the Dryland Sustainable Landscapes Impact Programme (DSL-IP),
> which… Later, DSL-IP child projects…

Applied across the platform on 2026-07-14. Each page is treated independently —
a visitor may land on any page, so "first mention" is per page, not site-wide.

## Where the rule does NOT apply

Expanding everywhere breaks the copy. These are deliberately left as bare acronyms:

- **Headings, card titles, nav, the topbar and the hero wordmark** — a spelled-out
  name overflows the layout and reads wrong.
- **Figure captions, photo credits, citations and reference lists** —
  `(APA, 7th ed.)`, `CC BY-SA 4.0`, `WOCAT Questionnaire on SLM Approaches (2019)`.
- **Author/affiliation lines** — `Drawn from the FAO / ICARDA…`, `Stefan Strohmeier (ICARDA)`.
- **Eyebrows and metadata strips** — `DSL-IP · FAO · GEF-9`.
- **Possessives** — "FAO's work", not "…of the United Nations (FAO)'s work". The
  expansion moves to a later plain mention.
- **After an article** — "an FAO report" is correct; "an Food and Agriculture…" is not.
- **Inside a parenthetical** — no nested brackets.
- **Runs of acronyms** — `NDCs, NAPs, NBSAPs and LDN targets` stays as it is. Expanding
  one member yields a half-expanded list. If such a run is a page's only mention, the
  acronyms stay bare and it needs an editor, not a script.
- **`design-system/*`** — internal component specs, not FAO-facing copy.

## Not acronyms

`APA` (citation style), `CC` / `BY-SA` (licence), `RAIN` (a partner organisation, and
also a word in `RAIN = DRY SEASON`). Do not expand these.
