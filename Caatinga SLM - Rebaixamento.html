Rebaixamento

Coppicing forage trees to bring browse within reach

What it is

Rebaixamento is the manual lowering (coppicing) of forage trees and shrubs to bring browse within animals’ reach, improve diet quality and extend green leaf into the dry season. About 60 percent of the system’s biomass then comes from the woody layer (Araújo Filho, 2013). It is well suited to thickets rich in palatable woody species – roughly 70 percent of Caatinga woody plants are forage (Araújo Filho, 2013).

How it works

  • Coppice recognised forage species: sabiá, jurema-preta, mororó, quebra-faca, pau-branco, feijão-bravo, camaratuba
  • Cut by hand; keep the riparian vegetation across the drainage network
  • First admit animals in the middle of the first rains after coppicing
  • Manage regrowth; take ≤60 percent of the forage (Araújo Filho, 2013)

Key facts

Available forage
~4× untreated Caatinga
Cattle gain
99.6 vs 80.6 kg/head/yr
Woody species that are forage
~70%
Best for

Figures from Araújo Filho (2013).

Note

Probably the most broadly suitable manipulation for the varied caatingas of the sertão, because so many of the woody species are palatable.
