Water Harvesting · Micro-catchment

Vallerani mechanized micro-catchments

A tractor-drawn plough that cuts crescent micro-basins at landscape scale

What it is

The Vallerani system uses a tractor-drawn “delfino” plough that cuts a continuous line of intermittent crescent micro-basins along the contour. It mechanizes semi-circular and contour bunding, so large degraded rangelands can be restored fast and cheaply – the plough does by the hectare what would otherwise be slow hand labour (Oweis, 2001).

In the WOCAT classification it belongs to the micro-catchment water-harvesting group: each pit is a small catchment-and-basin that ponds runoff for a planted shrub or tree. It has been adopted at landscape scale, notably in Jordan’s Badia Restoration Programme.

How it works

  • Pull the special plough on the contour (often laser- or GPS-guided)
  • The plough forms intermittent water-harvesting pits and ridges as it works
  • After the first runoff, plant nursery shrub or tree seedlings into the wet pits
  • Protect from grazing for ≥ 2 seasons, then graze to carrying capacity
