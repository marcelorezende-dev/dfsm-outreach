Peer-Farmer Learning

The experiential, farmer-to-farmer engine of the SLPF. Knowledge here is co-created, not imposed — farmers learn by doing, in their own fields, from each other. On the ground, this module is delivered through the Farmer Field School programme.

01

Learning by doing, together

Most extension tells farmers what to do. Peer-Farmer Learning lets them discover it — then teach the next farmer.

This module transfers proven and innovative SLM/SFM practices through context-sensitive, participatory methods. Rather than a one-way technology hand-off, it builds farmers’ capacity to diagnose, analyse and respond to land degradation themselves — linking hands-on technical training to landscape objectives.

Because the knowledge is co-created, adoption tends to endure beyond the project: a robust exit strategy is built in, and practices spread through the community’s own social networks rather than depending on outside agents.

02

How the module works

What it builds

  • Practical techniques to raise productivity while conserving landscape integrity.
  • Farmers’ own capacity to diagnose and respond to degradation.
  • Community cohesion and adaptive capacity through shared problem-solving.
  • A farmer-to-farmer cascade that keeps spreading after the project ends.

Where it sits in the SLPF

  • CSB supplies the germplasm farmers experiment with.
  • FFF / value chains reward the practices with market access.
  • Peer-Farmer Learning is the human engine that moves the skills.
  • Contextualized for each landscape through the ICDIP.
The delivery vehicle

Peer-Farmer Learning is realised on the ground as the Farmer Field School (FFS) programme — seasonal, field-based learning cycles on demonstration plots. That is the next level down.
