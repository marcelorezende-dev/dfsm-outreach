Water Harvesting · Macro-catchment

Check dams

Small barriers across gullies that slow flow, trap silt and recharge

What it is

Check dams are small barriers – of stone, gabion or concrete – built across gullies and small channels to slow runoff, trap sediment and recharge the bed. They reduce erosion while storing and spreading water in the channel itself.

Built as a series down a gully, they step the flow down gently, each one ponding water that infiltrates and building a fertile silt bed behind it.

How it works

  • Key the dam firmly into the bed and banks of the gully
  • Build a series down the channel so each ponds and infiltrates runoff
  • Provide a spillway/overflow so floods pass without breaching the dam
  • Let trapped silt build a fertile, moist bed that can itself be cropped

Site them with care

A case study from Karnataka, India (Batchelor & James) warned that poorly sited check dams and nala bunds can capture water that downstream users depend on – RWH structures must be planned at watershed scale, not field by field.

Related forms

  • Nala bunds – earthen bunds across small streams (India).
  • Gabions – wire-caged stone check structures for stronger flows.
  • Silt traps – small barriers that hold back sediment in canals and fields.
