2 · Description of the Approach

2.1Short description
A participatory, farmer-to-farmer extension approach that disseminates sustainable silvopastoral management of the Caatinga through demonstration units, multiplier training, community organisation, fit-for-purpose credit and a simplified licence – anchoring the practice in land users' own knowledge (Araújo Filho, 2013).
2.2Detailed description

The Approach takes the silvopastoral Technology from research stations to producers' land. Its backbone is participatory, experiential extension (camponês–camponês / farmer-to-farmer), organised around demonstration units on real holdings where land users see thinned, coppiced and enriched Caatinga across the dry and wet seasons.

It deliberately builds on traditional knowledge – the vaqueiro's and fundo de pasto communities' deep familiarity with which plants feed animals in drought – and engages youth as future multipliers. Research institutions (Embrapa, UFCG) generate and demonstrate the evidence; revitalised public extension (EMATER) and NGOs (IRPAA) carry it to communities; community associations and cooperatives organise collective work, markets and access to credit.

The Approach also works on the enabling environment: matching credit to the long forest cycle (PRONAF-Floresta, PRONAF-Agroecologia, FNE-Verde), Payment for Environmental Services / Bolsa Verde, and – critically – a single, simplified, declaratory licence built on the Rural Environmental Registry (CAR), with fee exemptions for family farmers and indigenous, quilombola and traditional communities, so land is not left idle awaiting permits. The whole is integrated with agroecology (Araújo Filho, 2013).

3 · Aims and enabling / hindering conditions

3.1Main aims / objectives
  • Scale sustainable silvopastoral Caatinga management among producers
  • Build land-user and extensionist capacity through participatory methods
  • Strengthen community organisation (associations, fundo de pasto)
  • Remove finance and licensing barriers to adoption
  • Integrate the practice with agroecology and traditional knowledge
3.2Conditions enabling (+) or hindering (–) implementation
Condition+/–Note
Social/cultural norms and values+Strong fundo de pasto collective tradition; deep drought knowledge
Availability/access to financial services–Credit poorly matched to the forest cycle → use PRONAF-Floresta, FNE-Verde
Institutional setting (extension)–Under-resourced public extension → revitalise and integrate EMATER with research
Collaboration / coordination of actors±Strong where research + extension + associations align
Legal framework (tenure, licensing)–Untitled land and complex licensing → simplified declaratory licence on CAR
Policies±PRONAF historically didn't cover pastoral Caatinga management
Land governance / tenure security–Insecure tenure deters long-term investment
Knowledge of SLM / technical support–Few trained extensionists → multiplier training, specialist roster
Markets±Firewood, meat, honey, fruit markets variable; sanitary inspection gaps
Workload / labour–Cutting and roço labour; out-migration → group labour (mutirão)

5 · Capacity building and activities

5.1Capacity building / training
farmer-to-farmer (camponês–camponês) demonstration units field days courses exchange visits
For land users, field staff and students; trains multipliers focused on Caatinga management for livestock.
5.2Advisory service
Extension delivered on demonstration units and farms by EMATER / IRPAA – integrated with research and education.
5.3Institution strengthening
Community associations and cooperatives formed/strengthened; revitalisation of public rural extension.
5.4Monitoring and evaluation
Participatory; field indicators – forage availability, regrowth, stocking, soil cover, income.
5.5Research
On-station and on-farm trials (Embrapa, UFCG) – the source of the Technology's quantitative data (Araújo Filho, 2013).

7 · Impact analysis and concluding statements

7.1Impacts of the Approach
Did the Approach…Rating
Empower land users and improve decision-makingYes, moderately
Build SLM / land-degradation knowledgeYes
Strengthen community institutionsYes
Improve livelihoods and drought resilienceYes
Reach scaleLimited – constrained by extension capacity
7.2Main motivation of land users
increased production / profit reduced drought risk payments / credit community rules and enforcement environmental awareness
7.3Sustainability of activities
Land users can sustain the practice itself (low external inputs), but diffusion at scale depends on continued participatory extension, fit-for-purpose credit and a workable licence.
7.4 – 7.5Strengths and weaknesses

Strengths

  • Builds on land users' own knowledge – high ownership
  • Demonstration units make benefits visible
  • Couples technique with finance, organisation and policy

Weaknesses → remedy

  • Thin extension capacity → train multipliers; farmer-to-farmer
  • Licensing and credit gaps → CAR-based declaratory licence; PRONAF-Floresta
  • Slow, project-dependent scaling → embed in permanent policy and PSA
