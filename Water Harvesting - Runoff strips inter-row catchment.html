Water Harvesting · Micro-catchment

Runoff strips (inter-row catchment)

Alternating bare catchment strips and cropped strips down the slope

What it is

Runoff strips are alternating strips of bare, compacted “catchment” land and cropped land running down the slope, so that each cropped strip receives the runoff generated by the bare strip just above it. The bare strip sheds water; the cropped strip catches and stores it – a simple form of inter-row catchment for field crops in very dry areas (Oweis, 2001).

In the WOCAT classification the technique sits in the micro-catchment water-harvesting group, where a short bare catchment feeds an immediately adjacent planted strip.

How it works

  • Alternate catchment and cropped strips along the contour
  • Set the catchment : crop strip-width ratio to the rainfall deficit – drier sites need wider catchments
  • Smooth or compact the catchment strip to induce runoff
  • Use small corrugations to spread the water evenly across the cropped strip
